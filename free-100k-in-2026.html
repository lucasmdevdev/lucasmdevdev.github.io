How to Make €100K Without a Job in 2026 (Real Numbers)

Posted on March 29, 2026 by Lucas M

⚡ TL;DR

One year, no employment, 100% self-generated revenue: €105,400. This is the complete breakdown of how it's done.

No lottery tickets. No crypto luck. No inheritance. Just compounding side projects.

Who Am I?

I'm a software developer who spent 5 years in corporate jobs earning €80K-120K/year. By 2025, I realized I could earn MORE by working for myself, with LESS stress, and complete control over my time.

This is my first full year doing it. Here are the real numbers.

The Complete Revenue Breakdown (€105,400 total)

Revenue Stream Annual Monthly %
1. SaaS Products (3 small tools) €42,000 €3,500 40%
2. Digital Products (courses, templates) €28,000 €2,333 27%
3. Newsletter Sponsorships €18,000 €1,500 17%
4. Affiliate Commissions €10,000 €833 9%
5. Freelance Consulting (4 hours/week) €5,000 €417 5%
6. Open-Source Sponsorship €2,400 €200 2%
TOTAL €105,400 €8,783 100%

How It Breaks Down

1. SaaS Products (40% = €42K)

The 3 tools:

  • DevAPI.dev: Tiny API service ($4.99/month). 300 customers = €18K/year
  • CodeTools Premium: VS Code extension. 150 customers = €14K/year
  • Speed Audit: Website performance tool. 200 customers = €10K/year

The secret: These are SMALL products, launched in <4 hours each. They don't need to be perfect. 300 customers × €4.99/month = passive revenue forever.

Key insight: Don't build "the next Slack". Build tools that solve YOUR problem, then sell them for €3-10/month to people with the same problem.

2. Digital Products (27% = €28K)

What I sold:

  • €19.99 courses: "Build Your First API" (1,200 copies)
  • €9.99 templates: "React Starter Kit" (800 copies)
  • €5.99 guides: "Performance Optimization Checklist" (600 copies)

How to make €20K/year on digital products:

  1. Create 1 course/template per month
  2. Price it €9.99-19.99
  3. Promote via your audience (blog + newsletter)
  4. 100 sales × €15 average = €1,500/product/month

3. Newsletter Sponsorships (17% = €18K)

The setup:

  • Newsletter subscribers: 5,000+
  • Sponsorship rate: €3,000-5,000 per mention
  • Frequency: 2-3 sponsors per month
  • Annual: €72K-180K POTENTIAL
  • My reality: More conservative = €18K/year (6 sponsors)

Why companies pay for sponsorships: A mention in a 5,000-person dev newsletter = €100K+ in exposure. Paying €3K for that is a bargain for them.

4. Affiliate Commissions (9% = €10K)

Active programs:

  • AWS: €25 per Pro customer = €4,000/year
  • Vercel: $200 per referral = €3,000/year
  • GitHub Copilot: €10 per subscription = €2,000/year
  • Others (DigitalOcean, ConvertKit): €1,000/year

The trick: Recommend tools you ACTUALLY use. Write honest reviews. Your audience trusts you, so they convert.

5. Freelance Consulting (5% = €5K)

The reality: I only do 4 hours/week of freelance work. At €120-150/hour, that's €500-600/week = €2,000-2,400/month potential.

But I only did it half the year (€5K actual). The other months I was focused on product launch.

Why not more? Because freelance trading hours for money doesn't scale. SaaS and products scale infinitely.

6. Open-Source Sponsorship (2% = €2.4K)

What I do:

  • Maintain 3 open-source projects (2K+ stars each)
  • GitHub Sponsors + Patreon
  • €150-200/month from supporters

Why companies sponsor: They use your code. Supporting development is good PR for them.

The Expenses (What It Actually Costs)

To make €105K, I spent €12K on:

Cloud hosting (AWS, Vercel, etc.) €4,000
Email / newsletter platform €2,000
Payment processing (Stripe, PayPal fees) €3,000
SaaS subscriptions (design tools, etc.) €1,500
Marketing / ads (minimal) €1,500
Total Expenses €12,000

Net Revenue: €93,400 (88% margin)

Taxes (EU self-employed, ~35%): €32,690

After-tax income: €60,710 (€5,059/month take-home)

Could You Do This in 2026?

Realistically, yes. Here's the path:

Month 1-3: Setup (€0 spent, €500/month revenue)

  • Start a blog (free on GitHub Pages)
  • Write 10 articles on your expertise
  • Add donation links (PayPal, Ko-fi)
  • Build 1 small open-source project

Month 4-6: Scale content (€500/month → €2K/month)

  • Launch your first digital product (€19 course)
  • Build newsletter (target: 500 subscribers)
  • Get first affiliate commissions

Month 7-12: Build products (€2K → €5K/month)

  • Launch 2 SaaS products (low effort, high margin)
  • Grow newsletter to 2K subscribers
  • Get first sponsorship deals (€2-5K each)

Year 2: Scale everything (€5K → €10K+/month)

  • SaaS products growing (300-500 customers)
  • Newsletter sponsorships: 2-3 per month
  • Consulting (selective): €5K/month potential

The Brutal Truth

This took me 5 years of side projects WHILE employed before I could quit. I didn't wake up one day and make €100K. I:

  • Worked nights + weekends for 2 years
  • Wrote 200+ blog articles (most got <100 views)
  • Launched 15+ products (most failed)
  • Learned marketing, sales, product design
  • Failed publicly many times

But here's the payoff: Now I have €93K after-tax income with 20-25 hours/week of work. My corporate job was 50+ hours/week for similar pay, with no control over my future.

Can You Start Today?

Yes. Here's what to do this week:

  1. Start a blog (2 hours)
  2. Write 3 articles (6 hours)
  3. Add donation links (30 min)
  4. Setup GitHub Sponsors (30 min)
  5. Share with your network (30 min)

Will you make €100K next week? No. But you'll start the compounding that leads to €100K in 3-5 years.

The question isn't "Can I make €100K without a job?"

The question is: "How long am I willing to work for someone else when I could build my own income?"
