I Quit My €120K Job to Build €100/Month Side Income. Here's Why (It Wasn't Crazy).

Posted on March 29, 2026 by Lucas M

The Setup

3 months ago, I was a senior developer at a Fortune 500 company. €120K salary, stock options, the works. Last month, I left to focus on passive income. Everyone thought I'd lost my mind.

This is the story of why I did it, and why the math actually works.

The Problem With the €120K Salary

My salary seemed great on paper:

  • Gross: €120,000/year
  • After taxes (42%): €69,600/year = €5,800/month
  • Living expenses (rent, food, utilities): €1,800/month
  • Savings: €4,000/month

Sounds solid, right?

But here's what wasn't mentioned:

  • Commute: 40 min each way = 1.3 hours/day × 250 working days = 325 hours/year wasted
  • Mentally draining meetings: 15 hours/week of stakeholder updates, sprint planning, 1:1s
  • Stress: On-call rotations. Pager alerts at 2 AM. Performance reviews.
  • Burnout velocity: My energy was decreasing month by month

At year 5 of the same job, I realized something: I was trading 40 hours/week for €5,800/month, but I was actually working 50+ hours/week when you count commute, stress, mental overhead.

That's €110/hour (gross), but after taxes? €65/hour for increasingly soul-crushing work.

The Side Income Opportunity

Meanwhile, I'd been building side projects for 2 years:

  • A blog: 50 articles, 5K monthly visitors
  • Digital products: Dev starter kits, API templates
  • Open-source sponsorships: 3 popular repos
  • Newsletter: 1,200 subscribers
  • Dev tools: Small APIs with freemium model

Current monthly revenue from side projects:

  • Donations: €40/month
  • Digital products: €30/month
  • Open-source sponsorship: €20/month
  • Affiliate commissions: €15/month

Total: €105/month (goal was €100)

At first glance, this looks insane. Go from €5,800/month to €105/month?

But wait...

The Real Math

Current living situation (with job):

  • Salary after tax: €5,800/month
  • Living expenses: €1,800/month
  • Dev tools, subscriptions, etc.: €200/month
  • Savings: €3,800/month
  • Actual lifestyle income: €2,000/month (what I actually spend on myself)

Post-job living (bootstrapped):

  • Side income: €105/month
  • Savings buffer: €50K from previous years
  • Living expenses: €1,600/month (no commute, no work clothes)
  • Runway: €50K ÷ €1,600 = 31 months

Even if I never made another euro, I could live comfortably for 2.5 years.

But here's the key: I freed up 10+ hours/week that I can now use to grow the side income.

What I'm Doing With the Extra Time

Before (with job): 2 hours/week on side projects (evenings + weekends)

Now: 30+ hours/week on scaling the business

Projects I'm launching in Month 1:

  1. New content course: "Build Your First API" (€19/year, targeting 500 sales = €9,500)
  2. Premium newsletter: Deep dives on dev careers (€5/month, targeting 200 subscribers = €1,000/month)
  3. Consulting side: €150/hour for 4-5 hours/week = €2,400-3,000/month
  4. Dev tools growth: Monetize existing projects better (target: 10x current €15/month)

Realistic projection for Month 6: €2,500-3,500/month

Realistic projection for Year 1: €3,000-5,000/month

And unlike the job, this is scalable. In Year 2, with better leverage, I could hit €10K+/month without increasing hours.

The Real Reasons I Did This

It's not just about money. Here's the full picture:

Factor Job (€120K) Bootstrap (€100/month now)
Time freedom 2 hours/week 40+ hours/week
Stress level High (on-call, reviews) Low (self-directed)
Growth potential €5-10K/year (title bump) €20-50K+ (scalable)
Skill development Deep in one stack Broad (marketing, sales, product)
Long-term control Company owns your time You own everything

The Risks (Real Talk)

I'm not pretending this is risk-free. Here's what could go wrong:

  • Revenue doesn't scale: I'm stuck at €100-500/month. (Mitigated by: €50K runway, ability to contract work at €100+/hour)
  • Burnout on side projects: Building products sucks when it's your only income. (Mitigated by: I can do part-time contract work)
  • Market changes: My audience interest shifts. (Mitigated by: Diverse revenue streams, skills still in demand)
  • No benefits: No health insurance from employer. (Mitigated by: European healthcare, private insurance €50-100/month)

But honestly? The downside risk is manageable. The upside is unlimited.

Why This Matters (Beyond My Story)

Here's why I'm writing this publicly:

Most developers don't realize they have options.

You don't need to:

  • Choose between a soul-crushing job or starving
  • Work 50 hours/week to afford rent
  • Ask permission to take time off
  • Wait for a promotion to increase your income

You can build an alternative. It takes 2-3 years of side project work WHILE employed, but you can do it.

The developers who are winning in 2026 aren't the ones with the biggest salaries. They're the ones with diversified income, time freedom, and ownership of their work.
